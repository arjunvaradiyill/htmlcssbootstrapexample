Top Destinations

Munnar

Munnar is a picturesque hill station located in the Idukki district of the Indian state of Kerala. It is renowned for its sprawling tea plantations, rolling hills, and mist-covered valleys. Situated at the confluence of three mountain streams, Munnar offers a unique blend of natural beauty and serene landscapes. The region is known for its rich biodiversity, including the rare Neelakurinji flowers that bloom once every twelve years and the Nilgiri Tahr, a species of mountain goat found in the Eravikulam National Park. Munnar’s cool climate, lush green surroundings, and tranquil atmosphere make it a popular destination

Kolukkumalai

Kolukkumalai is a remote village in Bodinayakanur Taluk, Theni district, Tamil Nadu, India. It is renowned for housing the highest tea plantation in the world, situated at an elevation of about 2,200 meters (7,200 feet) above sea level. This unique altitude imparts a distinct flavor and freshness to the tea grown here. Kolukkumalai is characterized by its breathtaking panoramic views of the surrounding hills and valleys, lush green tea estates, and a cool climate that enhances the tea’s quality. The area’s rugged terrain and serene environment make it a popular destination for tea enthusiasts and nature lovers alike.

Bekal Fort

Bekal is a coastal town in the Kasaragod district of the Indian state of Kerala. It is famous for the historic Bekal Fort, which is the largest fort in Kerala and offers stunning views of the Arabian Sea. The fort, with its strategic location and architecture, has served as a defense station throughout history. Bekal is also known for its beautiful beaches, such as the Bekal Beach and Kappil Beach, which are characterized by golden sands, swaying palm trees, and calm waters. The serene backwaters and the cultural heritage of Bekal, combined with its natural beauty, make it a charming destination for travelers.

About Kerala

Kerala is a southwestern coastal state of India known for its stunning landscapes and rich cultural heritage. It stretches along the Malabar Coast and is bordered by Karnataka, Tamil Nadu, and the Arabian Sea. The state's unique culture has been shaped by its diverse history and geographical location.
